Haves and have-nots: A theory of economic sufficientarianism

with Christopher P. Chambers

Journal of Economic Theory, 2024

This paper extends the philosophical concept of sufficientarianism to multi-good allocation problems. We propose that social welfare depends primarily on ensuring sufficient consumption for as many individuals as possible. We introduce and formalize "sufficientarian judgment"â€”the principle that when equal consumption becomes socially worse by changing one agent's bundle, no other agent's change can improve social welfare. We demonstrate that sufficientarianism is characterized by this principle combined with symmetry and separability, and establish its connection to the leximin criterion.
